# STR-007 Porter Five Forces — Optimaks Competitive Strategy

| Purpose | Assess competitive pressure in the Singapore SME digital automation, web agency, CRM, FSM and service workflow market. |

---

## 1. Strategic Summary

Optimaks operates in a high-competition market. The correct response is not to compete broadly.

The correct response is:

```text
Narrow the battlefield.
Own a specific service SME workflow.
Start with aircon / field service.
Sell Website-to-Workflow, not generic digital transformation.
```

---

## 2. Force 1 — Existing Rivalry: High

Existing competitors include:

```text
- web design agencies
- SEO / digital marketing agencies
- PSG vendors
- CRM vendors
- FSM SaaS providers
- ERP vendors
- service marketplaces
```

### Risk

If Optimaks positions itself as generic website design, CRM, automation or digital transformation, it becomes one of many.

### Strategic Response

```text
Do not compete as another web agency.
Compete as the practical Website-to-Workflow system for service SMEs.
```

Use the distinction:

```text
Web agencies stop at enquiries.
FSM software starts after the job exists.
Optimaks connects enquiry to job completion.
```

---

## 3. Force 2 — Threat of New Entrants: High

AI tools, templates, no-code platforms and freelancers lower the barrier to entry.

### Risk

Prospects may think:

```text
Anyone can build a website or automation now.
```

### Strategic Response

Build defensibility through:

```text
- industry workflow templates
- documented delivery SOP
- reusable Service OS modules
- aircon-specific demo
- scope and QA discipline
- client workflow review methodology
```

The moat is not only code. The moat is the combination of workflow understanding, productized delivery and implementation governance.

---

## 4. Force 3 — Threat of Substitutes: High

Clients can substitute Optimaks with:

```text
- WhatsApp
- Excel / Google Sheets
- existing admin staff
- cheap website freelancers
- PSG-supported software
- generic CRM / FSM SaaS
- marketplaces
```

### Risk

Clients may not see the cost of their current manual system.

### Strategic Response

Sell the cost of the current workflow:

```text
lost enquiries
slow quote follow-up
manual booking errors
delayed invoice tracking
forgotten maintenance reminders
no customer database ownership
```

Positioning phrase:

```text
Your current system is already WhatsApp + Excel + memory. Optimaks makes it trackable.
```

---

## 5. Force 4 — Buyer Bargaining Power: High

SME owners are budget-sensitive and compare across grants, freelancers, SaaS tools and existing vendors.

### Risk

Clients may negotiate heavily or request unlimited customization.

### Strategic Response

Use pricing ladder and package boundaries:

```text
Starter = Website + Lead Capture
Growth = Lead-to-Booking Workflow
Pro = Service Workflow OS
```

Use limited early offers carefully:

```text
Founding Client Offer = validation tool, not permanent price.
Diagnostic Offer = sales entry, not a low-price full system.
```

---

## 6. Force 5 — Supplier Bargaining Power: Medium

Suppliers include:

```text
- hosting / deployment providers
- Supabase or database platform
- WhatsApp API provider
- domain and email services
- short video or creative partners
- third-party SaaS integrations
```

### Risk

Over-reliance on one tool can raise cost or reduce flexibility.

### Strategic Response

Design around:

```text
core workflow + replaceable integrations
```

Avoid hard-coding provider-specific logic when a provider abstraction is practical.

---

## 7. Five Forces Summary

| Force | Intensity | Meaning for Optimaks |
|---|---:|---|
| Existing Rivalry | High | Do not compete broadly as web / CRM / ERP. |
| New Entrants | High | Build workflow templates and delivery system, not only code. |
| Substitutes | High | Sell the cost of manual WhatsApp + Excel workflow. |
| Buyer Power | High | Use package boundaries and value-based positioning. |
| Supplier Power | Medium | Keep integrations replaceable where possible. |

---

## 8. Strategic Rules Derived from Five Forces

1. Do not fight the entire SME digital transformation market.
2. Do not sell website-only as the core business.
3. Avoid PSG subsidy battles as the main sales argument.
4. Use the aircon / field service beachhead to narrow rivalry.
5. Package scope clearly to reduce buyer pressure.
6. Keep the product architecture modular and integration-flexible.
