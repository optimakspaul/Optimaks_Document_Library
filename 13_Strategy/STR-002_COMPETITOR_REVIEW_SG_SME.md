## 1. Competitor Category Model

Optimaks should classify the market into five groups:

| Category | Examples | Strategic Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| Web / Digital Marketing Agencies | FirstCom, Verz, OOm, MediaPlus, APEC, Novage | Compete for website, SEO, landing page, and lead generation budget. Usually weak after lead capture. |
| FSM / Field Service SaaS | FieldCamp, GenicTeams, SMITIV, Adaptive FSM, FacilityBot | Compete for job dispatch, scheduling, invoicing, and field workflow budget. Usually weak before lead capture. |
| Heavy CRM / ERP | HashMicro, Deskera, Salesforce, Globe3 | Alternatives for larger SMEs. Too heavy for many small service teams. |
| Marketplace / Lead Platform | Coolfix and similar service platforms | Not direct software competitors, but can own customer relationships and compress service provider margins. |
| Advanced Maintenance / AI / IoT | Groundup.ai and industrial maintenance AI providers | Mostly not direct competitors; useful signal that maintenance automation is a valid direction. |

---

## 2. Direct Competitor Set

Optimaks' direct competitors are those that compete for the same first digital budget from SME owners.

### Direct Competitor Type A — Web / Digital Marketing Agencies

They compete for:

```text
- website redesign budget
- SEO / SEM / online presence budget
- landing page / lead generation budget
```

Optimaks should avoid saying only:

```text
We build websites.
```

Better message:

```text
A normal website stops at enquiry. Optimaks continues the workflow after the enquiry.
```

### Direct Competitor Type B — FSM / Field Service SaaS

They compete for:

```text
- job scheduling
- dispatch
- job cards
- invoicing
- field operations management
```

Optimaks should avoid competing as a full FSM SaaS from day one.

Better message:

```text
Most FSM tools help you manage jobs after you already have the customer. Optimaks helps you capture the enquiry and move it into the job workflow.
```

---

## 3. Indirect Competitors / Alternatives

Heavy ERP / CRM providers are alternatives, not the initial battlefront.

Recommended comparison language:

```text
If Salesforce or ERP feels too heavy for your 5-10 person service team, Optimaks gives you a lighter first step.
```

Marketplace / lead platforms are not direct software competitors, but they create a strategic sales angle:

```text
Build your own brand, customer database, and repeat-service flow instead of depending only on a platform.
```

## 4. Competitor Positioning Map

Axes:

```text
X-axis = Front-end Lead Generation capability
Y-axis = Back-end Workflow Automation capability
```

Map:

```text
Back-end Workflow Automation ↑

High  |                       Heavy ERP / CRM
      |                  HashMicro / Deskera / Salesforce
      |
      |        FSM SaaS
      | FieldCamp / GenicTeams / SMITIV
      |
      |                         OPTIMAKS
      |              Website + Lead + Workflow
      |
      | Web Agencies
      | FirstCom / Verz / OOm / Novage
      |
Low   | Marketplace / Basic Listing
      | Coolfix / directories
      +------------------------------------------------→
        Low Front-end Lead Gen              High Front-end Lead Gen
```

Optimaks should occupy:

```text
Medium-high lead capture + medium-high workflow automation + low implementation complexity + fast launch.
```

---

## 5. Key Differentiation

```text
Web agency = front-end only.
FSM SaaS = backend only.
ERP / CRM = too heavy.
Marketplace = does not build owned customer asset.
Optimaks = owned website-to-workflow system for service SMEs.
```
