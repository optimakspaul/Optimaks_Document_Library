## 1. Core Statement

Optimaks does not build generic SaaS products.

Optimaks builds industry-specific Workflow Automation Systems and SME Operating OS solutions.

Each system must be designed around the client's actual daily workflow, not around abstract software features.

中文核心：

> Optimaks 不做通用 SaaS。我們為特定產業打造可落地的營運 OS，從接單、報價、排程、施工、收款到客戶回流，協助 SME 把日常流程系統化。

---

## 2. Definition of Optimaks WAS

WAS means Workflow Automation System.

An Optimaks WAS is a practical system that helps an SME owner reduce repetitive coordination work and make operations visible.

It may include:

1. Lead capture
2. Customer enquiry handling
3. Quote generation
4. Booking and scheduling
5. Job assignment
6. Job execution status
7. Proof of completion
8. Invoice and payment tracking
9. Maintenance reminders
10. Customer reactivation and retention
11. Monthly value / system health reporting

A WAS may contain CRM, ERP, FSM, booking, invoicing, reporting, and automation elements, but it should not be described only as one of those categories unless the project scope is intentionally narrow.

---

## 3. Workflow First, Feature Second

Optimaks projects must begin with workflow discovery before feature planning.

The correct sequence is:

```text
Industry
↓
Real daily workflow
↓
Pain point / bottleneck
↓
Small Core MVP
↓
Data model
↓
Page map
↓
Automation points
↓
Client value proof
↓
Add-on modules
```

The incorrect sequence is:

```text
Feature wishlist
↓
Large system prompt
↓
AI generates too much code
↓
Unclear data model
↓
Unclear delivery scope
↓
Difficult maintenance
```

---

## 4. Human Support, Not Full Human Replacement

Optimaks WAS is not positioned as a promise to fully replace human staff.

The purpose is to:

1. Reduce repetitive admin coordination.
2. Make work status visible.
3. Reduce missed follow-ups.
4. Help owners manage jobs, leads, quotes, and customers with fewer manual steps.
5. Create measurable value from monthly maintenance or subscription service.

Admin Executive, operations staff, technicians, and owners may still remain part of the workflow. The system should reduce friction and manual repetition, not pretend that all human judgment disappears.

---

## 5. Industry-Specific Operating OS Principle

Optimaks should prioritize vertical workflows where operations are repeatable and painful enough to justify systemization.

Good-fit industries usually have:

1. Repeated customer enquiries.
2. Quotation or service pricing steps.
3. Booking or scheduling needs.
4. Field service or job execution tracking.
5. Completion proof or status reporting.
6. Invoice or payment follow-up.
7. Maintenance, renewal, or repeat customer potential.
8. Owner visibility problems.

Aircon OS is the first example of this principle.

---

## 6. Relation to Small Core First

Every WAS project must start with a Small Core MVP.

The Small Core should prove the most important workflow before expanding into a full operating system.

Example for an aircon service company:

```text
Small Core:
Lead → Customer Profile → Quote → Job Status → Follow-up Note

Later Modules:
Booking Schedule → Technician Job Card → Completion Photo → Invoice Tracker → Maintenance Reminder → Client Portal
```

---

## 7. What Should Not Be Placed in the Constitution

Market research and competitor details should not be placed inside the core constitution unless they become repeated governance principles.

The following should usually remain in sales, proposal, project, or market research documents:

1. Microsoft Dynamics 365 pricing comparison.
2. JobStreet / SEEK job posting analysis.
3. Admin Executive salary comparison.
4. Competitor pricing.
5. Short-term sales scripts.
6. Package price experiments.
7. One-off market observations.

The constitution should capture the principle, not every market note.

---

## 8. Constitution-Level Rule

A project should not be approved as an Optimaks project unless it can clearly define:

1. Target industry.
2. Real workflow.
3. Operational pain point.
4. Small Core MVP.
5. Data owner and data flow.
6. Monthly or measurable value proof.
7. Expansion modules.
8. Client delivery boundary.

If these cannot be defined, the idea should remain as research or exploration, not become an implementation task.
