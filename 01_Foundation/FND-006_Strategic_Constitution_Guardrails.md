## 1. Why This Exists

This document prevents Optimaks from becoming an unfocused one-person custom agency.

Without guardrails, Optimaks may drift into:

```text
- generic web design
- social media management
- heavy ERP / CRM implementation
- unlimited custom software
- PSG-grant-driven price competition
- industry-agnostic positioning that sounds too broad
```

The purpose of this document is to convert approved strategic analysis into constitution-level decision rules.

---

## 2. Core Company Positioning Rule

Optimaks should be described as:

```text
A lightweight Website-to-Workflow system provider for Singapore service SMEs.
```

Optimaks helps service SMEs turn:

```text
Website / Google Maps / Short Video / WhatsApp enquiry
↓
Lead Capture
↓
Quote
↓
Booking
↓
Job Card
↓
Invoice Status
↓
Reminder / Follow-up
```

into a traceable workflow.

---

## 3. Negative Positioning Rule

Optimaks must not primarily position itself as:

| Incorrect Positioning | Why Not |
|---|---|
| Web agency | Pulls Optimaks into a red ocean with low differentiation. |
| Social media agency | Creates high-service workload, weakens workflow-system positioning. |
| Heavy ERP / CRM vendor | Triggers comparison with larger platforms and raises complexity expectations. |
| PSG reseller | Competes on grants and subsidies rather than speed-to-value and practical workflow. |
| General custom software shop | Encourages scope creep and one-off builds. |

---

## 4. Approved Strategic Focus

```text
External focus = Aircon / Field Service first.
Internal architecture = Reusable Service OS core.
```

This means:

- Sales and landing pages can be aircon-first.
- Product modules must remain reusable across service industries.
- Aircon OS is the first template, not the only long-term product.

---

## 5. Lanchester Beachhead Rule

Optimaks should follow a weak-player / small-battlefield strategy.

First beachhead:

```text
Singapore aircon and field service SMEs with 3-10 workers or technicians using WhatsApp, Google Maps, Excel, and manual admin to manage enquiries and jobs.
```

Optimaks should not initially fight the broad market:

```text
All SME digital transformation
All web design
All CRM
All ERP
All social media marketing
All PSG software
```

---

## 6. Feature Scope Rule

Approved core workflow:

```text
Enquiry → Lead → Quote → Booking → Job Card → Invoice Status → Reminder
```

If a feature does not support this workflow, it must be classified before implementation:

| Classification | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Core | Required for Website-to-Workflow delivery. |
| Add-on | Useful, sellable extension but not core MVP. |
| Future Version | Good idea, not now. |
| Partner Service | Better handled by collaborator or vendor. |
| Change Request | Client-specific request requiring scope, cost and timeline review. |
| Rejected | Not aligned with Optimaks positioning. |

---

## 7. Short Video / Social Media Rule

Short video should be packaged as:

```text
Short Video Lead Kit
```

Not as:

```text
Full social media management
```

Optimaks may provide:

- monthly video topics
- hooks
- short scripts
- captions
- CTA mapping
- shooting checklist
- lead source tracking

Optimaks should not promise:

- viral results
- guaranteed leads without paid acquisition and tracking control
- unlimited revisions
- daily posting
- full production team deliverables unless separately scoped

---

## 8. Pricing Guardrail

Pricing should support productized delivery:

```text
Starter = Website + Lead Capture
Growth = Lead-to-Booking Workflow
Pro = Service Workflow OS
Add-on = Short Video Lead Kit
Diagnostic = Sales Entry Offer
```

Pricing must avoid:

```text
- unlimited customization
- permanent underpricing
- competing only against PSG subsidies
- selling website-only work as the main business
```

## 9. AI Execution Guardrail

AI outputs must be checked against this document before entering implementation.

Codex / Antigravity must not add:

- marketplace matching
- complex ERP modules

## 10. One-Person Team Operating Model

Optimaks operates as a founder-led, AI-assisted, documentation-driven productized service company.

The founder acts as Product Owner, Sales Lead, Delivery Manager, and Final Quality Gatekeeper.

AI tools and external partners are not decision makers. They are execution and support layers governed by the Optimaks Constitution.

### Role Boundaries

| Role / Tool / Partner | Primary Responsibility | Boundary Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Paul / Founder | Product Owner, Sales Lead, Delivery Manager, final quality gatekeeper | Makes final decisions on positioning, scope, pricing, delivery and release readiness. |
| ChatGPT | Strategy, documentation, constitution review, scope control, prompt design | May recommend, structure and review; must not replace founder approval. |
| Gemini | Market research, vendor research, competitor digest | Produces research digest only; raw output must be reviewed before strategy or implementation. |
| Codex | Code implementation based on approved GitHub Issues | Executes scoped issues only; must not decide product direction or expand scope. |
| Antigravity | UI prototyping and app page generation based on approved scope | Builds or iterates UI only within approved product and data boundaries. |
| GitHub | Source of truth for code, documents, issues, versions, and decision records | Anything not committed or tracked is not official. |
| Vercel | Preview, demo, staging, and production deployment gate | Preview must be reviewed before production deployment or client delivery. |
| Supabase | Database, authentication, backend foundation, and data security layer | Schema, RLS and production data changes require approved scope and review. |
| External marketing partners | Lead generation support only | They may support traffic, content or campaign execution, but not product strategy ownership. |
| External developers/designers | Execution partners only | They must work from approved scope, documentation, issues and review gates. |

### Operating Chain

```text
Paul decides direction and final acceptance
↓
ChatGPT converts ideas into strategy, scope, documentation and prompts
↓
Gemini supports research when needed
↓
GitHub records approved issues, docs and decisions
↓
Codex / Antigravity execute approved scope
↓
Vercel / Supabase provide deployment and data foundations
↓
Paul reviews before merge, deployment or client delivery
```

### Acceptance Rule

All AI and partner outputs must be reviewed against the constitution before being accepted, merged, deployed, or delivered to clients.

## 11. Decision Checklist

Before accepting a feature, package, client request, AI output, partner output or landing page claim, ask:

1. Does this support Website-to-Workflow positioning?
2. Does this help the service SME core workflow?
3. Does this strengthen the aircon / field service beachhead?
4. Can this become a reusable Service OS module?
5. Is this core, add-on, future version, partner service, CR or rejected?
6. Does this risk turning Optimaks into a generic web / social / ERP agency?
7. Is this documented before implementation?
8. Has the founder reviewed and accepted the output before merge, deployment or client delivery?

If the answer is unclear, do not implement directly. Create a strategy review or change request first.
